# Unusual Merchant Category

Certain merchant categories carry materially higher chargeback and fraud rates in our historical data, particularly card-not-present categories like online retail, digital entertainment/gaming, and gift card purchases, where the merchant cannot visually verify the cardholder. Fraudsters favor these categories because goods can be resold quickly or consumed instantly, and because the transaction leaves no physical paper trail. A charge in a high-risk category is not automatically fraud, but when paired with an amount or location pattern that breaks from the cardholder's history, it should be treated as a stronger signal than the same anomaly in a low-risk category like groceries or utilities. Category risk should always be read in combination with the cardholder's own purchase history rather than as a blanket category-level rule.

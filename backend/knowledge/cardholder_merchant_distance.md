# Large Cardholder-Merchant Distance

A large gap between a cardholder's registered home location and the merchant's location is a moderate but reliable fraud indicator, since most legitimate spending clusters tightly around where a person lives, works, and regularly travels. Cloned or stolen card numbers are frequently used far from the original cardholder, either because the fraudster is physically distant or because the transaction was placed online from another region entirely. That said, this signal needs context: a cardholder with a recent travel booking, a relocation, or a pattern of cross-border purchases should not be flagged purely on distance. Distance should be weighted more heavily when it appears alongside an unfamiliar merchant or a timing anomaly than when it stands alone.

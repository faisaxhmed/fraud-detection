# Merchant Identity and Repeat-Offender Patterns

Beyond category, specific merchant identity matters: a small number of merchants account for a disproportionate share of confirmed fraud cases, often because they are easily impersonated, operate primarily online, or have weak point-of-sale verification. When a transaction's merchant has previously appeared in confirmed fraud cases at elevated rates, that history should raise scrutiny even if the amount and timing look otherwise unremarkable. Note that the raw dataset historically prefixed many merchant names with the literal string "fraud_" regardless of actual fraud status — a labeling artifact unrelated to true risk — so that prefix must never be treated as a signal; only merchant identity itself, post-cleaning, is meaningful here.

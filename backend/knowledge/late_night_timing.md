# Late-Night Transaction Timing

Transactions posted between roughly midnight and 4 AM show a disproportionately high fraud rate compared to daytime activity, even after controlling for amount and merchant category. This pattern likely reflects that compromised cards are tested and drained quickly, often by automated scripts or overseas actors operating outside the cardholder's waking hours, and that cardholders are less likely to notice or contest a charge while asleep. A late-night timestamp alone is weak evidence, but combined with an unusual merchant or a first-time amount it should raise the review priority meaningfully. Reviewers should also check whether the cardholder has a documented history of night-shift work or travel across time zones before treating the hour as suspicious.
